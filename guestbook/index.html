Our Guestbook!

When we realized we wouldn’t be able to host our family and friends, we still wanted to find a way to include everyone in our celebration and create a guestbook of sorts to memorialize this occasion and all the people who love and celebrate with us. And so began the great distributed craft project of Winter 2020, inspired by Homemade Emily Jane - How to make a signature memory quilt.

If you have found this page, you probably received a little package from us this December, including our holiday card, a white cotton square, a fabric pen and a card with instructions that read:

2020 has been anything but normal. Fittingly, in lieu of a tradiationl guestbook, Dana is making a memory quilt and hopes to include all of our loved ones with whom we couldn’t share our special day in person! Enclosed is a white cotton square and a fabric pen. Please write us a note, piece of advice, or just your signagure for our guestbook quilt. Be sure to write in the center of the sqaure and leave 1” from all sides to allow for trimming and sewing. Once signed, just slipe your square into the return envelope and mail it back to us; the pen is yours to keep! You can find more information about our wedding and this memory quilt project at DanaAndOwen.com

If these instructions seem a little fuzzy or confusing, never fear! In the following steps and pictures, I’m going to give you a little demo of how I will turn your signature square into a beautiful quilt block which should hopefully help resolve any lingering confusion. And if you feel you’ve messed up or you’d like another block to sign just shoot us an email, cotton squares are easy to mail (just hold on to that pesky fabric pen!)

Step by Step: Turning your square into a quilt block!

So when it arrived - your quilt block should have looked like this - neatly folded in a little bit of protective plastic - hold on to that! Once you are done signing you can slip it back in there to give it a little added protection on it’s way back to me.

So first thing to do is to pull it out and sign it. Please be careful to sign at least half an inch away from the side of the block. You were sent a 5” square block but all my pattern calls for is a 4.5” square. I will be trimming your block AND sewing with a quarter inch seam allowance.

A note on the pens: Everyone was sent either a blue pen OR a green pen. I scored a great deal on Black Friday to get these pens at a steep discount but there turned out to be slight differences between the two.

  • The blue pens have a larger brush tip and the ink flows faster. These can be easier to sign with naturally but if you write slowly they can bleed a little.
  • The green pens are finer tipped and work a little better for writing in small print and shading. But since they have a drier tip, You may find that you need to retrace your words or lettering to make them stand out.

No worries with either, I have plenty extra at home to help touch up any squares that need it and imperfections are what are going to make this quilt extra special!

Back to trimming! It’s more important to leave me space to trim and sew than to be centered, since as you can see in the next picture, I can trim your block center if given the necessary space!

This 4.5” CreativeGrids ruler is my very best friend for this part!

Now, each of the trimmed 4.5” squares will be the center of what’s known as a “sawtooth star”. After sewing with a .25” seam the center will be exactly 4”. I didn’t sew this square but have laid it over a previous example to demonstrate the size difference.

Here’s another example where I have scribbled in the seam allowance for clarity. If I sew it right, none of that black edge will ever show in the final block.

Instead I will end up with ~55 beautiful crisp sawtooth stars with sweet messages from all of you!

I haven’t finalized our fabric choices just yet but wouldn’t this sweet bundle from Cottoneer Fabrics be just perfect?!?

Stay tuned on this page for updates during the quilting process! Thank you all for loving us both over the years and celebrating with us now!
