Happy News!

On the 4th of December, socially distanced on the Seidel’s back deck in San Jose, CA, we got married in a virtual civil ceremony with the Alameda County clerk. We are thrilled to mark this new stage of our relationship but knew we had to find creative ways to include our loved ones near and far.

We put together this website to introduce each other to you all, our dearest family and friends, and share a little bit of our joy and plans for the future. While our ceremony is formally done, we plan to host a housewarming/anniversary party when it is safe to do so (and we have a house to host in!). Regardless, we look forward to many chances to celebrate with each of you over the course of what we hope is our long and happy marriage.

Read below for our story, pictures from our small nuptials, and information on how to sign our “guestbook” that was sent out with the wedding announcement.

The story, as told by Dana:

My friend Juliet once told me that it was “both suprising and so natural” to see Owen and I together. Surprising because we are such different people on the surface, but so natural how we fit together when you look a little deeper. Maybe that’s why it took us sometime ourselves to see that we should be together.

We first met at a birthday party for a mutual friend, Stacey, Owen’s roommate at the time, in July 2017. I spent most of the afternoon thinking Owen was actually Stacey’s husband and not minding for a minute that he listen in while I jabbered about dating horror stories with my girlfriends. Once set straight, I must have thought he was cute and interesting because I promptly sent Stacey a message…

On Owen, I made an impression (as I am want to do) but perhaps not one as alluring as I had hoped … it took us another year (almost to the date!) and another one of Stacey’s birthday parties for him to figure out that we should be dating.

Meet Owen

  • Favorite date night: Trying new beers at a local brewery

The story, as told by Owen:

It certainly took me a while to realize that Dana is the person who I should be dating! But luckily I joined her bookclub and our monthly meetings gave us time to become friends and get to know each other beyond the first impressions. Luckily for me, Dana’s hints stopped being so subtle after a while and once we were together, it was clear to both of us the match was perfect!

Dana’s outlook helps me to envision a myriad of future goals together. I am blessed to have her as my partner for the many adventures to come!

Our Wedding

On December 4, 2020, we joined our parents in an outdoor, socially distanced ceremony on Peggy & Glen Seidel’s back deck in Evergreen, CA. Other immediate family joined virtually and we had a brief but sweet ceremony with an enthusiastic county clerk over MSTeams. We exchanged rings and handwritten vows.

Click to see more photos on our Photos Page. If you would like to see a video of the ceremony, please reach out to us directly ♥.

Our Guestbook

While we are overjoyed to have the opportunity to get married this year, it was bittersweet to do so without all of our family and friends joining for the occassion. In a creative bid to include all those who missed out, Dana is creating a custom guestbook quilt, inspired by a fellow quilter.

Along with our holiday cards, we sent out 5” fabric squares for our loved ones to sign and return to us to form the basis of our guestbook quilt. For more instructions on how to sign the quilt squares, or to follow along for progress on the project, please see the Guestbook page.
